Abstract

A SOC analyst's job is mostly reading: logs, packets, alerts, and the gap between what a tool reports and what actually happened. This bootcamp builds that reading ability from the ground up, through labs that mirror the tasks a Tier 1 analyst is handed on their first week.

The ground covered is the durable part of the discipline — text and log handling, packet dissection and analysis, adversary simulation, and detection engineering. AI is integrated into the workflow rather than bolted on: where it speeds up triage, where it improves accuracy, and where trusting it will cost you.

Extensive hands-on work throughout. The emphasis is on skills that transfer between environments and tool stacks.

Who it's for

  • Aspiring SOC analysts preparing for a Tier 1 role.
  • Working analysts who want the fundamentals made solid.
  • IT staff moving into security operations.
  • Anyone being asked to fold AI into an existing SOC workflow.

What you'll be able to do

  • Triage security events and decide what is worth escalating.
  • Manipulate and search logs with grep, awk, sed, regex and PowerShell.
  • Dissect packet captures and find attack signatures in them.
  • Run adversary simulations and build detections from what they leave.
  • Use AI tools to speed up triage without inheriting their mistakes.
