Abstract

AI arrived on top of a workload that was already full. Most practitioners are asked both to threat-model the technology and to get useful work out of it, usually at the same time, usually without a map.

This course supplies the map. You build your own toolkit rather than renting someone else's, then point it at problems specific to your environment — generating detection rules from your own data, augmenting queries with retrieval, adding context to behaviour that is hard to read, and hunting patterns that are too diffuse to write a signature for.

It also covers the other direction: threat modelling AI systems, the frameworks used to govern them, and how the environment you just deployed gets attacked.

Who it's for

  • Detection engineers who want rules generated from their own data.
  • Threat hunters chasing patterns too diffuse for a signature.
  • Security engineers asked to threat-model an AI deployment.
  • SOC practitioners drowning in false positives.

What you'll be able to do

  • Build an LLM toolkit you own and can run yourself.
  • Augment queries using retrieval over your own data.
  • Generate YARA and SIGMA rules from your own telemetry.
  • Threat-model an AI environment and apply a governance framework to it.
  • Raise signal-to-noise by using AI to cut false positives.
