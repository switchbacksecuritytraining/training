Who it's for

Your first SOC role, or your next one

This training is built for IT, computer systems and network professionals moving into information security.

It's equally useful if you're already in the field and want to develop the specific skills and knowledge the job of a Security Operations Center analyst calls for.

What you'll
be able to do

Work a Security Analyst 1 desk

You'll leave with the skills and knowledge to work in a Security Analyst 1 job, and with a working understanding of how a Security Operations Center actually runs.

That includes the AI technologies now embedded in that work — what they do well, where they sit in the workflow, and how to use them without losing the fundamentals underneath.

Outline

Course outline

The course introduces the tools, AI-powered technologies and methodologies used in modern Security Operations Centers, blending traditional SOC practice with AI-driven platforms that improve detection, analysis and response.

Through extensive hands-on exercises and labs, you'll work through real SOC tasks using both industry-standard tools and emerging AI solutions.

Text and log handling

Working with the raw material of the job — the logs and text streams every investigation starts from.

Packet analysis

Packet dissection and analysis, down to the level where you can say what actually crossed the wire.

Adversarial simulation

Seeing the behavior you're meant to catch, generated deliberately, so detection work has something to aim at.

Detection engineering

Automated detection engineering: turning what you've learned about an adversary into something that fires reliably.

AI-assisted hunting

Threat hunting with AI support, plus the use of AI tools for automation, anomaly detection and alert triage.

The focus stays on practical, baseline skills augmented by AI rather than replaced by it — the combination that today's security environments actually ask for.

Before you
arrive

Prerequisites

An understanding of basic networking concepts, and basic Linux comprehension.

What to bring

A laptop with an up-to-date operating system and browser, and the ability to run virtual machines.

Check your laptop

Apple M-chip laptops are not compatible with the material used in this course. If that's your only machine, arrange a different one before the session.
